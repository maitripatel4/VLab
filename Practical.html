(A)Procedure to Determine Hardness

  1. Select the titrant
  2. Adjust the speed of the drops of the burette
  3. Adjust the molarity of titrant
  4. Choose the ion to be detected
  5. Select definite volume of water sample
  6. Choose indicator
  7. Start titration
  8. When color change occurs press stop and note the EDTA volume used!
  9. Then calculate the hardness of water in ppm using the following equations:

(B)Procedure to Determine Alkalinity

  1. Select the Titrant
  2. Select Normality
  3. Set proper speed of the drops
  4. Select titrate and its volume
  5. Select indicator, phenolphathalein to get pink color
  6. Press stop button when solution becomes colorless and calculate PA using the given equation.
    Assume A be volume of titrant used in Titration.
  7. Add methyl orange to same flask and continue the titration till color changes from yellow to orange and calculate TA.
    Assume B to be the total volume of titrant consumed by both indicators.
